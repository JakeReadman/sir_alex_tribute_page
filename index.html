Sir Alex Ferguson

The greatest Manager who ever lived

Here's a timeline of Sir Alex's life:

  • 1941 - Born in Glasgow, Scotland
  • 1957 - Begins his playing career with Queens Park as a striker, scoring 15 goals in 31 appearences.
  • 1967 - After plying his trade for St Johnstone and Dunfirmline Athletic after leaving Queens Park, Ferguson joins Rangers, where he scored 25 goals in 41 appearances.
  • 1974 - After finishing his playing career following spells at Falkirk and Ayr United, Ferguson takes on his first management position at East Stirlingshire. The spell is only a brief one and he moved on to St Mirren later that year.
  • 1978 - Ferguson then enjoyed a highly successful period as manager of Aberdeen, winning three Scottish league championships, four Scottish Cups and the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup in 1983. He briefly managed Scotland following the death of Jock Stein, taking the team to the 1986 World Cup.
  • 1986 - Appointed Manchester United Manager. During his 26 years with the club he won 38 trophies, including 13 Premier League titles, five FA Cups, and two UEFA Champions League titles. He was knighted in the 1999 Queen's Birthday Honours list for his services to the game. Ferguson is the longest-serving manager of Manchester United, having overtaken Sir Matt Busby's record on 19 December 2010.
  • 2013 - He retired from management at the end of the 2012–13 season, having won the Premier League in his final year.
